## Short Definition

A maze paradigm experiment is a psycholinguistic reading task where participants choose the correct next word of a sentence from two or more options at each step.

## Basic Idea

Instead of reading a sentence passively, the participant actively selects the word that best continues the sentence. The experiment records how quickly and accurately the participant makes each choice.

Example path:

```text
The / Quickly
student / cloud
read / jumped
the / blue
book / idea
```

Target sentence:

```text
The student read the book.
```

## What It Measures

- Reading-time difficulty at each word
- Accuracy of word selection
- Processing slowdowns at critical parts of a sentence
- Effects of ambiguity, agreement, word order, or semantic expectation

## Why It Is Useful

The maze paradigm keeps participants engaged because they must make a choice at every step. This can make it more sensitive than ordinary self-paced reading in some cases.

## Limitations

- The task is less natural than ordinary reading.
- Distractor words must be designed carefully.
- Poor distractors can make the task too easy or introduce unwanted cues.
- The task measures both language processing and decision-making.
